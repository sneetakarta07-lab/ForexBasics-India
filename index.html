How Forex Works (Simple Explanation)

Forex (foreign exchange) is where currencies are traded in pairs (example: EUR/USD). A price change reflects how one currency strengthens or weakens relative to another.

1) Currency Pairs

Example: EUR/USD shows the value of 1 EUR in USD. If the rate rises, EUR strengthened vs USD.

2) Spread

Spread is the difference between buy and sell prices. Lower spreads can reduce trading costs.

3) Leverage

Leverage can amplify gains and losses. Beginners should keep leverage low or avoid it until trained.

Beginner Safety Rules

  • Practice on a demo account for 1–2 weeks
  • Always set a stop loss
  • Avoid overtrading (too many trades per day)
  • Be careful during major news events (high volatility)
  • Ignore anyone promising guaranteed returns

How to Start (Step-by-Step)

Step 1 — Learn the basics (30–60 min)

  • Pairs, spread, pip, leverage
  • Risk basics: position sizing, stop loss

Step 2 — Practice on a demo account

  • Place 10–20 demo trades
  • Test one simple approach (trend + support/resistance)
  • Record results in a journal

Step 3 — Go small (only if you choose)

  • Start small to learn, not to “get rich fast”
  • Keep risk per trade low (e.g., 1% or less)
  • Stay consistent and avoid emotional decisions

FAQ

Is forex trading safe for beginners?

It can be risky, especially with leverage. Start with education, use a demo account, and follow strict risk rules.

Can anyone guarantee daily profits?

No. Legitimate platforms do not guarantee profits. Avoid anyone making guaranteed-return promises.

What’s the best time to trade?

Liquidity is often higher during London and New York sessions, but volatility may also be higher.
